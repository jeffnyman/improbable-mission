# Improbable Mission

_"Stay awhile ... stay forever!"_

In 1984, a maniacal villain taunted players from their Commodore 64 screens, and ["Impossible Mission"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impossible_Mission), a game published by [Epyx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epyx), became an instant classic. With its groundbreaking digitized speech, acrobatic platforming, and race-against-time puzzle-solving, it captivated a generation of gamers on the C64, ZX Spectrum, and other home computers of the era.

This repository is my attempt to bring that iconic experience to the modern web through a JavaScript reimplementation. My slight renaming of the game here is to ensure I avoid any suggestions of infringement. The use of "improbable" also reflects the likelihood of my actually being able to reproduce this game, particularly in JavaScript.

This project is entirely pedagogical (and, by the way, a vanity project!). Thus, my releases will initially focus on highlighting areas of significant work, such as displaying sprites and playing sounds, among other features. It will be a while before I have an actual working game. The current state of the project will always be available as a [running application on this repo](https://jeffnyman.github.io/improbable-mission).

# Progress

With my second "release" (and keep in mind this is a pedagogical project!), there were a few things I needed to prove to myself in the form of questions:

- Can I load sprites?
- Can I display sprites?
- Can I load sounds?
- Can I play sounds?
- Can I allow for movement?

Without any of that, this project was pretty dead in the water. With the second release, I had the entirety of that bare minimum!

The third "release" expanded on that by making sure that there was a player character (the agent) that the player could control. This allowed me to refine my logic for movement, sprite display, and audio playing.
